# Wins Log — Making Invisible Work Visible

Append-only log of shipped work, mentorship, glue work, and impact. The point is not heroics — it's that 6 months from now you will not remember what you did this Tuesday, and performance reviews / promotion conversations / CV updates / 1:1s reward people who *can* remember.

Full log lives at: `~/.ai-shared/wins/{year}.md` (one file per year, append-only).

## Entry Format

One to three lines per entry. Date first, then the win in the form **"did X, with Y impact / for Z reason."**

```
2026-05-15 — Shipped feature X. Pre-launch error budget held at 0.3%.
2026-05-14 — Closed long-standing auth contract ambiguity with BE team (2 sprints open).
2026-05-12 — Mentored junior through their first migration PR; they own the pattern now.
```

## What Counts as a Win

- **Shipped work with measurable impact** — feature, fix, perf improvement. Cite the metric if you have one.
- **Glue work** — closed a cross-team ambiguity, unblocked someone, wrote the doc nobody wanted to write.
- **Mentorship / sponsorship** — taught a principle in review, handed off a growth opportunity, ran a session.
- **Hard "no"s and scope wins** — talked a stakeholder out of an over-built feature, deferred a low-ROI task.
- **Tech vision moves** — landed an ADR, started a migration, made tech debt visible.

## What Does NOT Belong

- Routine ticket completion with no story behind it.
- Things you *plan* to do — only log what shipped or what happened.
- Vague self-praise ("worked hard this week"). Be specific.

## Cadence

- **Daily-ish:** capture as you go, especially right after a PR merges or a meeting resolves something.
- **Friday EOD (2 min):** scan the week, add anything you forgot.
- **End of quarter:** re-read the file before your 1:1 / review prep. Patterns will jump out.

## Why This Is Not Confluence / Notion

Friction kills logging. One markdown file, one append, no formatting overhead. If you ever need to publish a brag doc, you have the raw material to summarize from.
